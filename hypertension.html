Overview

Understanding the Condition

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a common condition in which the long-term force of the blood against your artery walls is high enough that it may eventually cause health problems, such as heart disease.

Signs & Symptoms

What to Look Out For

Most people with high blood pressure have no signs or symptoms, even if blood pressure readings reach dangerously high levels.

Headaches

Severe headaches, especially in the morning.

Nosebleeds

Frequent or hard-to-stop nosebleeds.

Vision Changes

Blurred vision or other visual disturbances.

Dizziness

Feeling lightheaded or dizzy unexpectedly.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing after mild exertion.

Chest Pain

Pain or pressure in the chest area.

Causes & Risk Factors

What Increases Your Risk?

Family History

Genetics play a crucial role in the likelihood of developing hypertension.

Age

The risk of high blood pressure increases as you get older.

Lack of Physical Activity

A sedentary lifestyle can lead to weight gain and higher heart rates.

Diet & High Sodium

Too much salt in your diet can cause your body to retain fluid.

Stress

High levels of stress can lead to a temporary increase in blood pressure.

Alcohol & Tobacco

Heavy drinking and smoking can damage your heart and blood vessels.

How We Diagnose

Accurate testing and monitoring

BP Monitoring

Regular checks using a sphygmomanometer.

ECG / EKG

Checks the electrical activity of your heart.

Blood Tests

To check cholesterol levels and kidney function.

Echocardiogram

Ultrasound waves to create images of the heart.

Urine Tests

To rule out conditions that can cause high BP.

Holter Monitor

24-hour monitoring of blood pressure and heart rate.
